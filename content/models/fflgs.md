## Overview
The Firefly FFLGS is the brand's SG-style double-cutaway solid body — reviewers frequently describe it as Firefly's closest copy of the classic SG shape, binding and all. It's an archive-heavy line: nearly all of its drop history predates the current tracking window, with dozens of finishes recorded in archived listings, including 24-fret versions, three-pickup versions, a 28"-scale baritone, relic runs, and even double-neck guitar-plus-bass builds sold with a hardcase.

## History
The FFLGS was one of the busiest lines in Firefly's earlier catalog — the archive shows it in transparent finishes, bursts, metallics, and novelty colors across a long run of drops. Its cadence has slowed in the current era, making back-catalog finishes effectively unobtainable except secondhand.

## Known quirks
Community feedback matches the wider brand pattern: expect to do a setup (fret polish, truss-rod tweak, action) before judging the guitar, after which fit and finish generally earn praise for the money. Stock pickups are the most commonly cited weak point — muddy under gain is the typical complaint — and they're the usual first upgrade. Unit-to-unit variance is the standing caveat.

## Buying notes
Because current drops are rare, most FFLGS hunting happens on the secondhand market, where condition and any prior setup work matter more than cosmetics. If a new drop does appear, note the configuration carefully — the name has covered everything from standard two-humbucker builds to baritones and double-necks — and expect it to sell through quickly.

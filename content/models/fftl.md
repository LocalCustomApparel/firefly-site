## Overview
The Firefly FFTL is the brand's T-style solid body. Listings describe ash or mahogany bodies depending on the run, roasted maple necks (plain or flamed), 9.5"–14" compound-radius fretboards, customized Alnico 5 pickups, Korea-sourced bridges with locking tuners, stainless frets, and a bone nut. Notable sub-variants include the "Mad Cat" — a tribute to the famous flamed-top guitar associated with Prince — plus semi-hollow and thinline P90 versions, baritone runs, and Classic Plus ash-body builds.

## History
The FFTL has run through many drop cycles with steady finish rotation: classic whites, sunbursts, paisleys, sparkle finishes, and striped novelty runs all appear in the archive. The Mad Cat drops in particular generated interest well beyond the usual Firefly audience because comparable tribute guitars from boutique makers cost several times more.

## Known quirks
Community feedback follows the brand pattern: arrival condition varies, and a setup — truss rod, action, fret-end cleanup — is the standard first step. Once dialed in, owners frequently describe performance above the price class. The stock single-coil-style pickups get reasonable marks for clarity; hardware spec (stainless frets, bone nut, locking tuners) is repeatedly cited as unusual at this price.

## Buying notes
The Mad Cat runs are the collectible end of this line and tend to move quickly — if that finish is the draw, buy on sight rather than waiting. For standard runs, check whether the specific drop is ash or mahogany-bodied and which pickup layout it carries, since both change the character of the guitar noticeably.

## Overview
The Firefly FFDB is the brand's reverse-body Firebird-style line. Most drops are six-string electrics — listings describe a mahogany body and neck, rosewood fretboard, Alnico 5 pickups, stainless ball-end frets, and a bone nut — but the name has also covered an explicit four-string bass version (mahogany body, Canadian maple neck), so the label spans both instruments. Spalted-maple-top runs are a recurring visual signature, alongside solids, bursts, and a related "FFDBN" naming variant in the archive.

## History
The FFDB appears in both the archive record and current drops, with the figured-top versions (blue spalted, sunburst spalted) drawing particular attention — reverse-body guitars with decorative tops are scarce at any budget price point. Community shorthand for the line is simply "the Firefly Firebird."

## Known quirks
Owner reports describe it as playing above its price, with the familiar brand caveats: a setup on arrival is expected, and stock electronics lean bright — the bridge pickup in particular gets called thin, making pickup swaps a common project. Repeat-buyer behavior shows up in owner threads, with people returning for a second unit in a different finish.

## Buying notes
Confirm from the listing whether a given drop is the guitar or the bass — the FFDB name has covered both. If the spalted top is the draw, remember each piece of figured wood is unique, so the photographed unit is indicative rather than exact. Reverse-body ergonomics (neck-heavy balance is common to the style) are worth factoring in if you've not played one.

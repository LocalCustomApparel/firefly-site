## Overview
The Firefly FFJA is the brand's offset, Jazzmaster-adjacent solid body. Current listings describe a mahogany body with a Canadian flamed maple neck, a 9.5"–14" compound-radius fretboard, Alnico 5 pickups, stainless ball-end frets, and a bone nut. The line includes standard six-string runs plus "VI" baritone variants — including a 24-fret version — in the long-scale, Bass VI-style tradition.

## History
The FFJA has appeared across many drop cycles, with archived colorways including flamed transparents, metallics, and sunbursts. The baritone VI runs recur often enough to be considered a fixture of the line rather than a one-off experiment.

## Known quirks
Owner reports rate the FFJA above comparable entry-level offsets for the money, with fretwork and tuning stability drawing consistent praise. The near-universal theme is that it needs a proper setup out of the box — buyers who do one report satisfaction, buyers who skip it report frustration. Stock pickups are the most commonly cited weakness, described as the natural first upgrade rather than a dealbreaker.

## Buying notes
The baritone VI variant is a large part of this model's appeal: long-scale offsets are otherwise a boutique-priced niche, and the FFJA VI is one of the cheapest ways to explore that territory for lower tunings or Bass VI-style parts work. Standard runs compete on the usual Firefly terms — strong spec for the price, setup required, electronics upgradeable. Check the specific listing for scale length and fret count, since the name covers both formats.

## Overview
The Firefly FFSP is a single-cutaway solid body in the vein of a Les Paul Special — the store's own catalog groups it in the LP family. Listings describe a mahogany body and neck, rosewood fretboard, Alnico pickups, stainless ball-end frets, and a bone nut. The line is unusually broad: P90 versions, three-pickup versions, flamed and spalted maple tops, semi-hollow "Vintage" variants, relic finishes, and even 28"-scale baritone runs have all shipped under the FFSP name.

## History
The FFSP is one of Firefly's most consistently active lines, with a steady stream of drops in both the live tracking data and the archive record. Novelty finishes are a signature of the line — crackle, bullseye, tiger stripe, dragon and snake inlays, and camouflage runs appear alongside standard bursts and solids.

## Known quirks
Community feedback follows the familiar Firefly pattern: strong fundamentals for the price, with out-of-the-box setup being the main variable. Neck feel and fretwork tend to draw praise; stock electronics are the most commonly upgraded component. Because the line spans so many configurations, the playing experience differs more between FFSP drops than it does for most other Firefly models.

## Buying notes
Read the listing closely — "FFSP" covers everything from a two-humbucker solid body to a P90 special to a semi-hollow or a baritone. If a specific inlay or finish appeals, treat it as a one-time run: the more elaborate colorways rarely return. As with the rest of the lineup, budget for a setup and consider the stock pickups a starting point rather than a destination.

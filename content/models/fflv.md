## Overview
The Firefly FFLV is the brand's Flying V-style solid body. Archived listings show it in a wide spread of finishes — bursts, transparents, metallics, walnut, olive green, and bat-inlay specials. Owner reports describe set-neck construction with a mahogany body, and the brand's usual hardware package of bone nut and stainless frets; reviewers repeatedly note that a set-neck V at this price is rare.

## History
The FFLV's drop history is concentrated in the archive record — it was a regular in earlier catalog cycles with at least eighteen distinct archived colorways, but it hasn't been a fixture of recent drops. That makes the secondhand market the main hunting ground for most finishes.

## Known quirks
Owner threads on this model skew more positive than the brand average, with fretwork and finish drawing consistent praise for the price. The stock ceramic pickups are rated merely adequate, and swapping them is the standard first upgrade. The V shape brings its own practical annoyances that owners mention — it won't sit on a knee like a conventional body, and the jack placement can call for an angled cable.

## Buying notes
The natural comparison reviewers make is with Epiphone's budget V offerings, and the FFLV generally holds its own on spec while undercutting on price. If you've never owned a V, factor in the ergonomics — playing seated requires the classical position or a strap. As with all archive-heavy Firefly lines, a specific finish reappearing is unlikely, so secondhand patience beats waiting for a re-drop.

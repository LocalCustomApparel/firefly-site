## Overview
The Firefly FFLX is a solid-body electric in the brand's extended single-cut/LP-adjacent family. Listings describe a mahogany body and neck, rosewood or maple fretboard depending on the run, Alnico pickups, stainless ball-end frets, and a bone nut. The line's distinguishing feature is its baritone variant: current baritone listings specify a 27" scale, one of the cheapest routes into baritone territory from any maker.

## History
The FFLX has appeared steadily across both the archive record and current drops, in the brand's usual rotation of bursts, transparents, sparkle-adjacent metallics, and matte finishes, plus occasional inlay specials like the bat-inlay run. The baritone version appears intermittently rather than every cycle.

## Known quirks
There's little model-specific discussion online — the FFLX name doesn't show up in reviews the way FFST or FFLPS do — so most of what's knowable comes from brand-level patterns: setup on arrival is expected, fundamentals (neck, frets, build) generally please for the price, and stock electronics are the first thing modders touch. Treat any strong claim about this specific model with skepticism until more owner reports accumulate.

## Buying notes
The baritone is the standout reason to watch this line — extended-scale guitars at this price are genuinely scarce, and for drop-tuned styles it's a low-risk experiment. For standard-scale FFLX drops, the value calculation is the same as the rest of the catalog: strong spec sheet, budget electronics, and a setup cost you should mentally add to the sticker price.

## Overview
The Firefly FFST is the brand's S-style solid body. Current listings describe a mahogany body with a roasted maple neck (flamed on some runs), a 9.5"–14" compound-radius fretboard in maple or rosewood, customized Alnico 5 pickups, Korea-sourced bridges, locking tuners, stainless frets, and a bone nut — a spec sheet that reads well above its usual price bracket. The line includes "Classic Model," "Classic Plus" (ash body), "Super," and factory-relic variants.

## History
The FFST has been in the lineup across many drop cycles, with the archive showing a wide range of finishes — paisleys, sparkles, chameleon color-shift paints, and relic'd whites and sunbursts alongside standard colors. Relic versions in particular have drawn attention in budget-guitar circles as an inexpensive way into the aged-finish look.

## Known quirks
The consistent community read is that the hardware and neck spec punch above the price, while unit-to-unit consistency is the gamble: some guitars arrive nearly gig-ready, others need real attention to nut slots, relief, and fret ends. Stock pickups are more polarizing than on some other Firefly lines — some owners find them perfectly usable, others swap them early.

## Buying notes
Reviewers usually benchmark the FFST against entry-level and mid-tier Squiers, and the spec-for-money comparison tends to land in Firefly's favor — with the caveat that a professional setup may eat some of the savings. Mahogany (rather than the traditional alder/ash) gives it a slightly different tonal character than the guitars it visually resembles, which some buyers count as a feature and others as a deviation.

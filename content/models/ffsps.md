## Overview
The Firefly FFSPS is the evolution of the FFSP single-cut line — the store's catalog groups both under its Les Paul family. Listings describe a mahogany body and neck, rosewood fretboard, Alnico 5 pickups, stainless ball-end frets, and a bone nut, with some runs built as semi-hollows with an F-hole. Like the FFSP, the line spans many configurations: three-pickup versions, spalted and birds-eye maple tops, locking-tuner runs, ebony-fretboard specials, and baritone variants.

## History
The FFSPS name took over much of the drop cadence that previously ran under FFSP, and it has become one of the most active current lines — the tracker regularly catches new FFSPS colorways. Finish variety is a hallmark: bat graphics, bullseye patterns, sparkles, Woden-god artwork runs, and standard bursts have all appeared.

## Known quirks
Community sentiment on this line runs more consistently positive than on some other Firefly families, with fit, finish, and fretwork drawing regular praise for the price. One specific recurring gripe: the Nashville-style bridge's saddle screws are held by spring tension and can work loose — owners mention careful adjustment or a dab of thread-locker as the fix.

## Buying notes
Certain colorways have developed minor cult status among repeat buyers and disappear fast when they return. Check whether the specific drop is solid or F-hole semi-hollow, and how many pickups it carries, since the FFSPS label covers both. The usual budget-import advice applies: plan on a setup, and treat any electronics upgrade as optional rather than assumed.

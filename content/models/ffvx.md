## Overview
The Firefly FFVX is an aggressive V-style solid body widely read as a budget take on the angular Dean ML/Washburn shapes associated with Dimebag Darrell. Listings describe a mahogany body and neck, maple or rosewood fretboard depending on the run, Alnico pickups, stainless ball-end frets, and a bone nut. Three-pickup versions and graphic finishes (camouflage, circles, "Snow Falcon") have appeared alongside standard colors.

## History
The FFVX shows up steadily in both the archive record and current tracking, with a mix of solid, burst, and novelty graphic finishes. It sits alongside the related VX Star — a star-shaped sibling sold under "VX Star" and "Woden God" names — in the brand's pointier, metal-oriented corner.

## Known quirks
Reviewer coverage is mostly YouTube-driven and aimed at the metal audience, with the recurring question being how much guitar a sub-$300 price can buy in this shape. Fret and neck feel generally rate well; the standing brand caveat about unit-to-unit consistency applies, and some reviews have flagged individual problem units. Most owner reports treat a setup on arrival as a given.

## Buying notes
This is a shape you buy because you want the look and the genre association — it rewards high-gain styles and stage presence more than couch practice, and like most V-family bodies it's awkward seated. For players wanting the Dime aesthetic without signature-model money, it's one of the few current options at this price; just note which pickup configuration and fretboard wood the specific drop carries.

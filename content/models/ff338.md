## Overview
The Firefly FF338 is a budget-friendly semi-hollow electric guitar built in the style of the classic double-cutaway 335-family instruments. It typically pairs a laminate body with a center block, dual humbucking pickups, and a Tune-o-matic-style bridge with stopbar tailpiece. It's aimed at players who want the look and tone of a semi-hollow without the price tag of established brands.

## History
Firefly Guitars sells direct-to-consumer through its own web storefronts rather than traditional dealer networks, which keeps costs down by cutting out retail markup. The FF338 has been a recurring model in the lineup, released periodically in limited batches and finish colors rather than kept continuously in stock — drops tend to sell out and later reappear with new color options.

## Known quirks
As with many budget imports, quality can vary between production batches, and owners commonly report wanting a basic setup (intonation, action, nut slots) shortly after arrival. Hardware and electronics are functional but modest for the price point, and some buyers choose to upgrade pickups, tuners, or wiring over time.

## Buying notes
Because Firefly sells in drops rather than maintaining continuous stock, timing matters — a specific finish may not return once it sells out, so it's worth checking current availability before assuming a color will still be listed. It's also worth budgeting for a setup after the guitar arrives, and comparing specs (pickup type, hardware finish, neck construction) across different drops, since details can change between production runs.

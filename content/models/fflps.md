## Overview
The Firefly FFLPS is the brand's single-cutaway, Les Paul-style solid body — by drop count, one of the biggest lines Firefly has ever run. The usual recipe is a mahogany body and neck with a maple top, rosewood fretboard, dual humbuckers, bone nut, and stainless ball-end frets, though pickup layouts vary widely between runs: archived drops include three-P90 versions, three-humbucker versions, F-hole variants, and inlay specials like the bat-inlay runs.

## History
The archive record shows the FFLPS (and its earlier "FFLP" naming) cycling through an unusually long list of finishes — bursts, sparkles, metallics, spalted-maple tops, and novelty colorways. It has been a fixture of the lineup across many drop cycles rather than a one-off, and it's often the model people mean when they talk about "the Firefly Les Paul."

## Known quirks
The recurring community theme is "good bones, budget electronics": the woodwork and finish generally get praise for the price, while pickups, pots, and tuners are the parts owners most often upgrade. Reported QC issues skew toward fixable things — high frets causing buzz, minor finish blemishes — with unit-to-unit inconsistency being the most repeated criticism.

## Buying notes
Reviewers tend to frame the FFLPS against entry-level Epiphone Les Pauls, generally favorably at its usual price point — the value equation weakens if you're paying resale markup. Plan on a setup when it arrives, and check the specific drop's pickup configuration carefully, since a "FFLPS" listing can mean anything from classic dual humbuckers to a three-P90 layout.

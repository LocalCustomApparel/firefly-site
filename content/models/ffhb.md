## Overview
The Firefly Hollow Body is a full-hollow electric guitar — unlike the semi-hollow FF338 family, there's no center block. It has appeared under several storefront names over the years, including "Full Hollow Body Guitar," "Crossroads," and a three-pickup "Switchmaster" configuration, all built on the same basic hollow platform. Listings describe mahogany or maple body and neck construction (varying by run), rosewood fretboards, Alnico pickups, stainless ball-end frets, and a bone nut.

## History
This is one of the most drop-active lines in the archive record — dozens of finish and pickup variants have cycled through the store since the brand's early days. The Crossroads and Switchmaster names appear in older archived listings, while recent drops tend to use plain "Hollow Body" or "FFHB" naming. Like all Firefly models, it sells direct in limited batches that sell out and reappear later in new colors.

## Known quirks
Owners and reviewers commonly note that units arrive needing a setup — bridge, saddle, and nut adjustments are typical first jobs. Reviewers have also pointed out the strong resemblance to other budget import hollow bodies sold under different brand names, suggesting shared factory origins. Unplugged resonance is a frequently mentioned pleasant surprise for a laminate build.

## Buying notes
Because it's fully hollow, feedback at high gain is more of a consideration than with the semi-hollow 338 models — this line suits cleaner and mid-gain styles. Pickup configuration varies meaningfully between drops (two humbuckers vs. the three-pickup Switchmaster layout), so check the specific listing. Budget for a setup on arrival, and if a particular finish matters to you, buy when you see it — colors rarely repeat quickly.
